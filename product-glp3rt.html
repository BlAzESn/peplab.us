Common Questions
Frequently Asked About GLP-3 (RT)
GLP-3 (RT) simultaneously activates GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1), GIP (Glucose-Dependent Insulinotropic Polypeptide), and glucagon receptors. This triple-agonist mechanism is what distinguishes it from dual agonists like tirzepatide, which targets only GLP-1 and GIP.
After reconstitution, store at 2–8°C (refrigerated) for up to 4–6 weeks, or at −20°C for up to 6 months. Do not refreeze after thawing. Lyophilized (dry powder) form is stable at −20°C for up to 24 months when kept sealed and dry.
GLP-3 (RT) has a molecular weight of approximately 4,751.4 g/mol and consists of 39 amino acid residues. Its molecular formula is C194H301N53O58. It is classified as a long-acting synthetic peptide analogue.
GLP-1 mono-agonists (like semaglutide) target only one receptor. GLP-3 (RT)'s additional GIP and glucagon receptor activity is hypothesized to provide complementary effects on fat oxidation, energy expenditure, and hepatic glucose regulation, areas that GLP-1 alone does not directly address.
